Three Things - Travel

7/4/15

  1. Going out and getting it done

    • Boyz On The Hoods

      Ride reports, Ramblings, Photos & Videos

      One of my favorite spots to look for folks doing fun things on bikes is boyzonthehoods.com, a blog about long bike rides (randonnees & brevets) in the bay area. Not updated all-too-often, it is nonetheless a source of bicycle inspiration.

    • #Microadventures

      by Alastair Humphreys (Vimeo album)

      Boyzonthehoods led me to a short video on Vimeo by Alistair entitled 'Mountain Biking & Bothy Nights.' My 20-year-old self would be rolling his eyes at the thought of some inspirational gobbledy-gook about going out and getting exercise and communing with nature. My 38-year-old self, however, is looking for whatever he can that will help him be more healthy and happy. 'Bothy Nights' is pretty great inspiration.

  2. Automated travel

    • Techdirt Podcast

      Episodes 28 & 29 on the future of travel

      I listen to this podcast regularly and highly recommend it to anyone interested in tech- especially the internet. Episode 28, 'Is Car Ownership On The Way Out?', focuses on how services like Uber and Lyft are changing the way we approach everyday travel. Interestingly, they also talk about types of travel that won't be impacted by such services. Episode 29, 'Autonomous Vehicles Will Change Everything', focuses on autonomous vehicles and their impact on travel and how we think about travel. Will we be ready to give up our cars when the technology is ready? How will autonomous travel fit in with the traditions of rambling and roving a la 'Easy Rider,' drives in the countryside and aimless meanderings?

    • 99% Invisible

      Episodes 170 & 171 Automation Paradox pts. I & II

      Another one of my regular podcasts, 99% Invisible, which focuses on design, also had a two-episode run about automated travel (airing the week after the Techdirt shows). Part I, 'Children of the Magenta', is about automatic pilots and Air France flight 477, which suddenly and mysteriously crashed in the Atlantic ocean. Part II, 'Johnnycab,' takes a look at Google's work on automating car travel. This is a nice compliment to the Techdirt podcasts because it explains the Google project in more detail, while the Techdirt show gets more into the cultural ramifications.
